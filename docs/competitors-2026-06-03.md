## Business Context

**FactoryLM / MIRA** — AI-powered industrial maintenance intelligence platform. Structures messy maintenance reality (manuals in filing cabinets, PLC tags, tribal knowledge) into a Maintenance Intelligence Namespace, then runs MIRA (AI copilot) on top. Serves manufacturing plants. Pricing: $500 assessment → $2–5K/mo pilot → $499/mo operating layer. 68,000+ chunks of OEM documentation indexed.

**CraneSync** — AI-powered field service assistant and real-time monitoring platform for industrial equipment maintenance, starting with overhead cranes.

## Key Strategic Observations

**Tractian is the closest MIRA analog to watch.** $200M raised, same AI copilot + CMMS pitch, now expanding globally. The key differentiation for FactoryLM is the namespace-first approach — MIRA's value is in structuring the plant's data layer BEFORE running AI on it. Tractian skips that with proprietary hardware.

**The CMMS market is commoditizing.** MaintainX, Limble, UpKeep, and Fiix are all adding AI features rapidly. FactoryLM must lead on OT grounding (PLC tags, UNS, manuals) — that's the moat CMMs players can't easily replicate.

**Konecranes is CraneSync's biggest threat.** They're the OEM, they own the service relationship, and TRUCONNECT gives them real-time data. CraneSync's wedge is brand-agnostic mixed-fleet environments and the field tech AI assistant layer.

**The connected worker angle is heating up.** Augmentir's Augie, MaintainX CoPilot, Siemens Operations Copilot — everyone is shipping an AI assistant for the floor. MIRA's differentiation is the grounded namespace under the hood, not just chat.
