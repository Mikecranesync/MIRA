## Your Business Context

**FactoryLM** (factorylm.com) — AI-powered industrial maintenance digital transformation. Turns messy maintenance reality (manuals in filing cabinets, mismatched PLC tags, tribal knowledge) into a structured **Maintenance Intelligence Namespace**. MIRA runs on top to give grounded, citation-backed diagnostics via Slack/Telegram/web. Offers a $500 floor assessment, pilot at $2–5K/mo, and an operating layer at $499/mo.

**CraneSync** (cranesync.com) — AI-powered field service assistant and real-time condition monitoring platform for industrial overhead cranes. Targets the $45B crane industry with IoT + AI service management. Patent-pending technology.

### 1. Tractian
**Website:** https://tractian.com  
**What they do:** Hardware + software + AI rolled into one "Industrial Copilot." Wireless sensors, AI diagnostics, and maintenance workflows in a single platform. Helps maintenance teams deploy predictive monitoring quickly without IT integration headaches.

## FactoryLM Differentiation Summary

Your strongest moats vs. this field:
1. **Namespace-first architecture** — UNS/ISA-95 structure before AI. Most competitors skip this and hallucinate.
2. **Evidence-grounded answers** — Every MIRA response cites manual §, work-order ID, or PLC tag. No competitor does this systematically.
3. **Assessment → Pilot → Operating Layer** — The $500 floor walk is a genius wedge. No other company leads with in-person data audit.
4. **68K+ OEM doc chunks already indexed** — 10 major brands. This training corpus is a moat.
5. **OT/IT bridge** — PLC tag reconciliation to asset namespace. Cognite does this for enterprise; you do it for mid-market in days.
