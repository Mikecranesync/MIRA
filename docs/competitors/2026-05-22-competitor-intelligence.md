## Business Context

**FactoryLM** turns messy maintenance reality — manuals in filing cabinets, mismatched PLC tags, tribal knowledge — into a structured Maintenance Intelligence Namespace. **MIRA** is the AI diagnostic assistant that runs on top. **CraneSync** is an AI-powered field service assistant and real-time monitoring platform for industrial cranes.

The competitive landscape spans five categories:

1. AI-First CMMS & Industrial Maintenance Platforms
2. Connected Worker / Factory Copilot Platforms
3. Enterprise Industrial AI & IIoT Platforms
4. Predictive Maintenance & Condition Monitoring
5. Crane / Heavy Equipment Monitoring

## Strategic Takeaways

1. **The namespace gap is real and uncontested.** No competitor offers a structured maintenance knowledge namespace as a service — the structuring layer (manuals, PLC tags, tribal knowledge, asset hierarchy) is either assumed to already exist or completely ignored. This is FactoryLM's moat.

2. **Everyone has an AI chatbot; nobody grounds it.** Tractian, Fiix, Augmentir, and others all have "AI assistants" — none cite manual page references or reconcile PLC tags to assets before answering. MIRA's grounding approach is unique.

3. **The $500 assessment as entry wedge is smart.** Most competitors require software commitment upfront. A $500 paid assessment with a written gap report is low friction and educates buyers at the same time.

4. **CraneSync has a specific window.** Konecranes TRUCONNECT is the 800-lb gorilla but is locked to Konecranes hardware. 60% of the 2M+ installed crane base has no predictive monitoring. An OEM-agnostic AI field service assistant for crane technicians is a clear gap.

5. **Key innovation angles to watch:** Edge-native PdM (no cloud latency), GenAI + first-principles engineering (like Siemens + NVIDIA), agentic maintenance workflows (like Augmentir's Agent Studio), and voice-AI for hands-free plant floor use (Datch).
