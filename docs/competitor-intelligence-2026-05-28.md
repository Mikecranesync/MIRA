## Business Context

**FactoryLM / MIRA** (`factorylm.com`) is an AI-powered industrial maintenance intelligence platform. It structures messy maintenance reality — manuals in filing cabinets, mismatched PLC tags, tribal knowledge — into a Maintenance Intelligence Namespace (UNS-compliant), then runs MIRA (the AI copilot) on top for grounded troubleshooting, fault diagnosis, and PM scheduling. Pricing: $500 assessment → $2–5K/mo pilot → $499/mo operating layer.

**CraneSync** (`cranesync.com`) is an AI-powered field service assistant and real-time monitoring platform for industrial equipment maintenance, starting with overhead cranes.

The space spans: industrial maintenance AI copilots, AI-powered CMMS, predictive/prescriptive maintenance, IIoT platforms, and asset-centric field service management.

## Strategic Takeaways

1. **The market is splitting into two camps** (as f7i.ai research confirms): high-end full-stack service providers (Augury, TRACTIAN, AspenTech) vs. agile interoperable platforms. FactoryLM lives in the second camp — and that's the right bet.

2. **No one else is structuring the namespace first.** Most competitors start with sensors or CMMS and bolt on AI. FactoryLM's "structure first, then MIRA runs on top" approach is architecturally differentiated.

3. **CraneSync's biggest direct threat is Konecranes** (OEM-tied) and Raygain (low-cost). CraneSync's moat: brand-agnostic, AI field service assistant layer (not just monitoring).

4. **MaintainX ($2.5B valuation) is the benchmark to watch** for Atlas CMMS. Their $150M Series D (July 2025) signals the market believes AI-native CMMS is a $B+ opportunity.

5. **Siemens is building the same thing** at enterprise scale. FactoryLM wins in plants Siemens doesn't reach (SMB, brownfield, sub-$1M annual budget).
