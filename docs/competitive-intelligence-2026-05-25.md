## Your Business Context

**FactoryLM / MIRA** (factorylm.com) — AI-powered industrial maintenance diagnostic platform. Turns messy maintenance reality (manuals in filing cabinets, PLC tags, tribal knowledge) into a structured **Maintenance Intelligence Namespace**, then MIRA runs on top as a Slack/Telegram-based copilot. Differentiated by the UNS gate (confirm asset context before troubleshooting), OEM documentation indexing (68K+ chunks), and grounded AI that cites manuals and work-order history.

**CraneSync** (cranesync.com) — AI-powered field service assistant and real-time monitoring platform for industrial equipment maintenance, starting with overhead cranes.

## Strategic Summary

| Threat Level | Companies | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 **Highest** | Edmund AI, Factory AI (f7i.ai), Makula | Direct overlap on AI + documentation + PLC diagnostic approach. Edmund just funded; f7i.ai is your most aggressive content competitor. |
| 🟠 **High** | Tractian, MaintainX, UpKeep, Limble | Well-funded, high-distribution CMMS + AI. Risk: "good enough" AI built into CMMS displaces the namespace structuring pitch. |
| 🟡 **Medium** | PTC ServiceMax AI, Fiix/Rockwell, Augury | Enterprise reach + AI copilot features. Fiix is notable because Rockwell owns the A-B ecosystem — your key OEM documentation target. |
| 🟢 **Watch** | Samsara, Siemens MindSphere, Hitachi Lumada | Platform expansion risk — they have the distribution; watching whether they build grounded maintenance AI or stay at the sensor layer. |

### Key Strategic Insights

1. **Edmund AI is your closest product analog.** They are executing the same PLC + documentation + AI diagnostic play in Europe. They're 6–12 months behind on funding but moving fast. Worth mapping their product roadmap carefully.

2. **Fiix/Rockwell is a channel threat.** Rockwell owns the Allen-Bradley/PowerFlex install base that is MIRA's primary OEM documentation content. If Rockwell bundles maintenance AI into the FactoryTalk ecosystem, it's a distribution moat.

3. **f7i.ai is your SEO/content competitor.** They produce buyers guides explicitly comparing platforms and ranking against MIRA's exact keywords. Study their content architecture.

4. **The CMMS players (MaintainX, UpKeep, Limble) are adding AI fast but remain shallow.** None of them enforce a UNS confirmation gate or ground answers in a structured namespace — that's the MIRA differentiator to hammer in positioning.

5. **CraneSync faces competition from Makula and MSI Data** in the OEM field service AI space. Makula in particular is well-positioned at machinery manufacturers doing aftermarket service.
