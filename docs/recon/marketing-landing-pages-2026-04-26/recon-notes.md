## Executive Takeaways

Factory AI is not winning on copy alone. It feels more polished because the page is tightly sequenced: clear category statement, immediate product proof, trust logos, quantified outcomes, product split, audience fit, testimonials, FAQ, then pricing. It removes friction from the first ask and makes the product feel already deployed in the reader's world.

FactoryLM already has the sharper product thesis. "The AI troubleshooter that knows your equipment" is more memorable than most competitor headlines, and the dark industrial look is directionally right. The gap is mostly packaging: trust proof arrives too late, the hero mockup does not feel fully alive, and the trial path asks for too much before the user gets a quick win.

Highest-leverage changes:

1. Keep the homepage thesis, but add stronger trust proof and a more believable product surface in the first viewport.
2. Turn `/cmms` into a fast trial funnel with passwordless magic-link entry.
3. Borrow Factory AI's page sequencing and MaintainX/UpKeep's trust-first layout habits.
4. Make the first CTA feel reversible and low-risk: "Start free", "No credit card", "Magic link", "See sample answer".
5. Use real product screenshots and tighter comparison blocks instead of leaving the hero to carry too much alone.

## Factory AI Homepage

What works:

- Very clear category framing: predictive maintenance + CMMS in one platform.
- Light theme, grid background, and big centered typography make it feel modern and expensive.
- Hero immediately drops into a real product shot, then logos, then value sections.
- The page tells a complete story in order: what it is, how it helps, measured value, product split, who it is for, customer outcomes, FAQ.
- Pricing is visible in top nav from the start.
- CTA is simple: `Get Started`. Sign-in is separated and low-noise.

What creates polish:

- Consistent spacing rhythm. Large sections, then compact proof modules.
- Actual customer logos early.
- Product screenshots are large enough to read as software, not decorative thumbnails.
- Audience sections and testimonials are concrete enough to feel like real deployments.
- Pricing supports the story with a free tier, trial language, and transparent packaging.

## FactoryLM Homepage

What is already strong:

- Best headline in the set for specificity and memory.
- Dark theme and amber accent suit industrial diagnostics well.
- The diagnostic chat mockup is on-message and immediately differentiates MIRA from generic CMMS tools.
- The below-the-fold sections are actually good: fault diagnosis, CMMS write-back, voice + vision, blog resources, and site-license framing.
- The "works with MaintainX, Limble, and UpKeep" line is strategically smart.

What currently holds it back:

- The first viewport feels sparse. There is a lot of negative space, but not enough trust proof to justify the emptiness.
- The main product mockup is credible, but it still reads like a static concept frame more than a living product.
- There are no customer logos or customer proof in the hero band.
- Secondary CTA `Read the blog` dilutes the main action too early.
- The first proof that this is already useful in a plant arrives later than it should.
- Navigation is thin for a serious software company. It does not yet feel like a fully developed commercial surface.

Design notes:

- The typography is good enough to keep.
- The dark shell is worth keeping.
- The fix is not "be lighter like Factory AI." The fix is "make the dark design feel more finished and more trusted."

## FactoryLM `/cmms` Trial Page

This page is the clearest place to improve conversion.

What works:

- The page is focused and message-aligned.
- It clearly explains the three-step value story.
- It reinforces compatibility with existing CMMS systems.
- It already looks like a dedicated trial or beta funnel rather than a generic contact form.

What is too heavy:

- The user hits a multi-field form immediately: work email, company/facility, first name, terms checkbox.
- The page asks for commitment before giving a quick payoff.
- There is no obvious "instant try" moment.
- It feels like a beta application, not a modern trial entry.

Recommendation:

- Replace the current top-of-page beta form with a passwordless magic-link start.
- First ask: only work email.
- After email entry: send magic link, then land the user in a seeded workspace or guided demo.
- Collect company/facility name, first name, and integration context after the user has seen value.

Ideal tester flow:

1. Homepage CTA: `Start Free` or `Get Early Access`.
2. Trial page asks only for work email.
3. Magic link lands the tester in a sample or newly created workspace.
4. First-run setup asks one thing at a time: plant name, primary equipment, current CMMS, manual upload.
5. Show one fast win immediately: ask a fault code, upload a manual, or inspect a sample diagnostic answer.

This is exactly where the passwordless note belongs. It should be part of the tester trial period, not buried later in account settings.

## What FactoryLM Should Copy First

### Homepage

- Add customer or partner logos directly under the hero.
- Replace or augment the hero mockup with a larger, sharper product surface.
- Add one short proof strip under the CTA:
  - works with MaintainX, Limble, UpKeep
  - cited OEM answers
  - photo-to-diagnosis
  - site license, not per-seat
- Promote pricing and trial clarity harder.
- Add one concrete customer-outcome block before the blog/resources section.

### Trial / Beta Flow

- Switch to magic-link authentication.
- Ask for email first, everything else later.
- Add `No credit card required` if true.
- Add a sample experience after login so the first session never feels empty.
- Make tester onboarding feel like product access, not lead capture.

### Visual System

- Keep the dark palette, but increase the amount of polished proof in the first two scrolls.
- Use one or two real plant photos or more realistic interface compositions, not only black-background product panels.
- Increase screenshot scale and readability.
- Tighten the hero vertical spacing so it feels intentional rather than empty.

## Concrete Design Backlog

1. Homepage hero refresh:
   - add trust logos
   - enlarge live product proof
   - simplify secondary CTA
2. Add testimonial or quantified outcome band above resources.
3. Rebuild `/cmms` around email-only magic-link entry.
4. Add a post-login sample workspace or guided demo path for testers.
5. Keep the pricing page, but link to it more assertively from the homepage.
6. Add one "how it works in 3 steps" strip to the homepage, not only `/cmms`.
7. Consider an UpKeep-style industrial photo section paired with MIRA overlays or UI cards.

## Bottom Line

FactoryLM does not need a new identity. It needs a more convincing first two minutes.

The winning move is to keep the industrial diagnostic positioning, then borrow:

- Factory AI's sequencing,
- UpKeep's real-world hero composition,
- MaintainX's trial clarity,
- Limble's dark-theme confidence,
- Fiix's credibility stacking.

If we implement only one funnel change first, make it this: homepage CTA to `/cmms` with passwordless magic-link trial entry and a seeded first diagnostic experience.
