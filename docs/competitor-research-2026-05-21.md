## Business Context

**FactoryLM** (factorylm.com) turns unstructured maintenance reality — manuals in filing cabinets, mismatched PLC tags, tribal knowledge — into a structured **Maintenance Intelligence Namespace**. MIRA, their AI agent, runs on top of that namespace to answer real grounded maintenance questions on the factory floor via Slack/Telegram.

**CraneSync** (cranesync.com) is an AI-powered field service assistant and real-time monitoring platform for industrial equipment maintenance, starting with overhead cranes.

The competitive space spans: AI-grounded maintenance assistants, IIoT platforms, CMMS with AI, predictive maintenance (PdM) services, and crane/field-service monitoring tools.

## Key Insights

1. **Nobody does the namespace wedge at SMB price points.** IBM Maximo Assist is the closest feature analog but costs 10-100x more and requires enterprise IT. FactoryLM's $500 assessment → $2-5K/mo pilot → $499/mo operating layer is a genuinely unoccupied position.

2. **Fluke is moving fast into "manuals → answers."** March 2026 eMaint AI launch is the most direct threat to FactoryLM's messaging. Watch their adoption curve closely.

3. **CraneSync has minimal direct AI competition.** PSIORI (Germany) and Versatile (construction) are the closest, but neither is doing field service AI for overhead cranes in the US the way CraneSync describes.

4. **The biggest PLG/sales opportunity is the CMMS buyers** — MaintainX, UpKeep, Limble customers are already sold on digital maintenance but have no AI grounding layer. FactoryLM can layer on top.

5. **Augmentir is worth a close watch** — they publish "Top Industrial Copilot Vendors" lists and are actively building the connected worker AI category. Partnership or competitive risk.
