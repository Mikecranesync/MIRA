# Competitor & Innovator Intelligence Report
**Date:** June 17, 2026

**Space:** Industrial Maintenance AI, AI-Powered CMMS, Predictive Maintenance, IIoT

---

## Your Business Context

**FactoryLM / MIRA** (factorylm.com) — AI-powered industrial maintenance platform. Turns messy maintenance reality (manuals in filing cabinets, mismatched PLC tags, tribal knowledge) into a structured *Maintenance Intelligence Namespace*, then runs MIRA on top to deliver grounded, cited troubleshooting answers to plant technicians. $500 floor assessment → $2-5K/mo pilot → $499/mo operating layer. 68,000+ OEM documentation chunks indexed. Chat interface via Telegram + Web + Slack.

**CraneSync** (cranesync.com) — AI-powered field service assistant and real-time monitoring platform for industrial overhead cranes. Niche play in equipment-specific AI.

### 1. Tractian
**URL:** https://tractian.com  
**Category:** Industrial AI Copilot + IoT Sensors + CMMS  
**Founded:** 2019 | **HQ:** Atlanta, GA  
**Funding:** VC-backed (undisclosed; strong growth)

The most direct competitor at the positioning level. Tractian calls itself the "Industrial Copilot" — fusing high-frequency IoT sensors with AI and a built-in CMMS to generate work orders from real-time physics-based machine data. Unlike legacy CMMS that are passive databases, Tractian acts as an active participant in maintenance strategy. G2 Leader in Industrial IoT, Asset Management, and Enterprise Asset Management in 2026. Strong in mid-market manufacturing. **Threat level: HIGH** — same "AI on the floor" pitch but with proprietary hardware sensors bundled in.

### 2. Augury
**URL:** https://augury.com  
**Category:** Machine Health Monitoring + Predictive Maintenance  
**Founded:** 2011 | **HQ:** New York, NY  
**Funding:** $369M total (Series F: $75M, Feb 2025) | **Valuation:** $1B+ (Unicorn)  
**Investors:** Lightrock, Baker Hughes, Insight Partners, Eclipse Ventures, Qualcomm Ventures, Munich Re Ventures

One of the first Industry 4.0 unicorns. Uses proprietary vibration and ultrasound sensors + a massive machine-sound database to predict failures in rotating equipment. Halo = wireless real-time asset monitoring device. Now expanding into "Production Health" beyond just machine health. Entry price ~$50K+/year. Deep enterprise client base (Colgate, Heinz, Frito-Lay). **Threat level: MEDIUM** — premium hardware-first model, priced out of your SMB market but validates the space heavily.

**Funding:** $254M total | **Series D:** $150M (July 2025) | **Valuation:** $2.5B

However, lacks deep PLC/OT integration and grounded manual namespace that MIRA provides.

## Key Takeaways for FactoryLM / MIRA

**Your white space:** No one else combines OT-native PLC/UNS namespace + document grounding + cited troubleshooting answers in a package that starts at $500 (assessment) with a path to $499/mo. The big players (IBM, SAP, Emerson) are too expensive and too slow. The CMMS players (MaintainX, Limble, UpKeep) have AI but no OT namespace. Tractian and Factory AI are the most comparable but are sensor-hardware-first, not document-namespace-first.

**Biggest competitive risks:** Tractian (if they add document/manual grounding), Factory AI (same motion, less OT depth), and the Rockwell stack (Fiix + Plex + Allen-Bradley) for all-in Rockwell plants.

**Prometheus is the wildcard** — $41B valuation, $18B in funding, Jeff Bezos, focused on industrial AI. Long-term this reshapes the entire space, but near-term it's focused on engineering and design, not plant-floor maintenance ops.
