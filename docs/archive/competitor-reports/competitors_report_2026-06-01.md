## Your Business Context

**FactoryLM / MIRA** (`factorylm.com`) is an AI-powered industrial maintenance intelligence platform. It structures a plant's "maintenance reality" — manuals, PLC tags, fault history, tribal knowledge — into a Maintenance Intelligence Namespace, then runs MIRA (an AI diagnostic assistant) on top. Serves plant maintenance technicians via Telegram, web, and CMMS write-back. Pricing: $500 assessment → $2–5K/mo pilot → $499/mo production.

**CraneSync** (`cranesync.com`) is an AI-powered field service assistant and real-time monitoring platform for industrial equipment maintenance, starting with overhead cranes.

**Combined space:** Industrial AI maintenance diagnostics, IIoT condition monitoring, AI-assisted CMMS, field service intelligence for asset-heavy manufacturers.

## Key Takeaways

**FactoryLM's white space:** No competitor combines (a) structured namespace ingestion, (b) a grounded AI chat assistant, and (c) a CMMS write-back at a price point accessible to a single-line plant. TRACTIAN and Augury dominate hardware; MaintainX and Fiix dominate CMMS; MIRA sits in the grounded-AI-chat-on-YOUR-data gap between them.

**CraneSync's white space:** Konecranes TRUCONNECT is the OEM incumbent but only covers Konecranes equipment. CraneSync's opportunity is the multi-brand crane fleet that no single OEM monitors. Closest competitors: Nanoprecise (sensors), Samsara (telematics), Makula (field service AI).

**Fastest-growing threat:** Factory AI (f7i.ai) is the most direct go-to-market overlap — sensor-agnostic, CMMS-inclusive, fast-deploy, mid-market. Watch closely.
