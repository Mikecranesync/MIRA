# Competitor & Innovator Research — Industrial Maintenance AI
**Generated:** 2026-05-30 | **Scope:** FactoryLM / MIRA + CraneSync competitive landscape

## Business Context

**FactoryLM / MIRA** turns messy maintenance reality (manuals in filing cabinets, mismatched PLC tags, tribal knowledge) into a structured **Maintenance Intelligence Namespace**, then runs MIRA (AI copilot) on top. Sells assessments ($500), pilot structuring ($2–5K/mo), and an operating layer ($499/mo). Inference: Groq → Cerebras → Gemini cascade.

**CraneSync** is an AI-powered field service assistant and real-time monitoring platform for industrial equipment maintenance, starting with overhead cranes.

**Competitive space:** AI-powered CMMS, predictive/prescriptive maintenance, industrial AI copilots, connected worker platforms, IIoT platforms with maintenance intelligence.

## FactoryLM / MIRA Differentiation

No competitor is doing the full **namespace structuring → grounded AI troubleshooting** motion at FactoryLM's price point:
- Enterprise players (IBM, AspenTech, Cognite) do this at $100K+/yr contracts
- CMMS players (MaintainX, Limble, UpKeep) have AI copilots but no structured namespace layer — answers aren't grounded in *your* specific plant
- Sensor-first players (Tractian, Augury) require hardware installation and don't solve the manual/tribal knowledge problem
- **FactoryLM's wedge:** $500 assessment → proven ROI → $499/mo operating layer. Nobody else is doing the on-the-floor structuring work at this price point for mid-market manufacturers.
