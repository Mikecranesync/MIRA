# FactoryLM / MIRA — Competitor & Innovator Intelligence
**Generated:** 2026-06-24 (automated scheduled task)  
**Business Context:** FactoryLM is the maintenance-context layer (Maintenance Intelligence Namespace) that makes messy factory data trustworthy for AI. MIRA is the diagnostic agent that answers technicians with cited sources from manuals, fault history, and PLC tags. CraneSync is an adjacent AI-powered field service platform for industrial crane maintenance. Both share the same core thesis: structure the data first, then AI can be trusted on the floor.

### 1. Tractian
**Website:** https://tractian.com  
**Category:** Industrial AI Copilot + Predictive Maintenance + CMMS  
**Stage:** Series C — $120M raised (Dec 2024), $700M valuation  
**Headquarters:** Atlanta, GA (offices in São Paulo, Mexico City)  

**What they do:** The closest single-stack competitor. Tractian combines proprietary vibration/temperature/runtime sensors, edge AI models, and a built-in CMMS in one unified platform. Claims to be "the only maintenance platform that fully combines CMMS workflows, real-time asset monitoring, and AI-driven automation." 500+ customers including John Deere, Bosch, Embraer, Hyundai. Strong in 1,000+ industrial plants.

**Threat to FactoryLM:** High. Sensors + software bundle is compelling for plants that want hardware included. Their CMMS also gives work-order write-back out of the box.

**Differentiation from FactoryLM:** Requires proprietary hardware sensors (increases upfront cost). Strong on rotating equipment vibration—weaker on OEM manual RAG, tribal knowledge capture, UNS structuring, and cited text-based diagnostic answers. FactoryLM doesn't need hardware; FactoryLM structures manuals and context, then deploys MIRA on top.
