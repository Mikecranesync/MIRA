# Competitor & Innovator Research — Industrial AI Maintenance
**Generated:** 2026-06-06  
**Scope:** Competitors and innovators in the industrial AI maintenance, predictive maintenance, AI CMMS, and crane field-service intelligence space — relevant to FactoryLM/MIRA and CraneSync.  
**Business context:**  
- **FactoryLM / MIRA** — structures maintenance reality (manuals, PLC tags, tribal knowledge) into a Maintenance Intelligence Namespace, then runs MIRA (an AI troubleshooting copilot) on top. Sells assessment ($500), pilot ($2–5K/mo), and operating layer ($499/mo).  
- **CraneSync** — AI-powered field service assistant + real-time monitoring for industrial cranes.

### 1. Augury
**Website:** https://augury.com  
**What they do:** Predictive maintenance via hardware sensors (vibration, temperature, acoustic, magnetic) plus an AI platform for manufacturing machines. Recently partnered with MaintainX to close the loop between prediction and work-order execution.  
**Why relevant:** Direct competitor on the "AI brain grounded in real asset data" story. Better-funded but more hardware-dependent than MIRA's software-first approach.  
**Key details:** ~$300M raised in VC funding. Described as "gold standard" for large enterprises needing white-glove managed service. Targets bearing wear, motor issues, HVAC.

### 2. Tractian
**Website:** https://tractian.com  
**What they do:** AI-powered condition monitoring sensors + a mobile-first CMMS. ML models auto-detect bearing wear, misalignment, cavitation, lubrication issues. All-in-one hardware + software.  
**Why relevant:** Closest in spirit to FactoryLM's wedge — combining asset intelligence with a CMMS and making it usable on the floor. Strong Y Combinator pedigree.  
**Key details:** Raised $15M Series A (Next47 + Y Combinator). Brazilian-founded, now US-focused. Targets mid-market manufacturers.

### 3. Factory AI (f7i.ai)
**Website:** https://f7i.ai

**Why relevant:** Positioning overlaps heavily — "industrial copilot" framing, brownfield-friendly, sensor-agnostic. Actively publishing content that dominates Google for MIRA's target keywords.  
**Key details:** US-based. No-code deployment. Strong SEO presence in the industrial AI maintenance space. Strong competition for organic discovery.

## Strategic Takeaways

**FactoryLM / MIRA differentiation:**
- Most competitors either sell hardware-first (Augury, Tractian) or are generic CMMS tools (MaintainX, Limble, UpKeep). The "structure-the-namespace-first, then run AI" approach is rare — Cognite and Palantir do it at enterprise scale, but no one is doing it for mid-market manufacturers at $499/mo.
- InSkill is the closest architectural competitor (grounded copilot + OEM procedures) but sells to OEMs, not plant operators.
- The "assessment → pilot → operating layer" sales motion is unique in the space.

**CraneSync differentiation:**
- Konecranes TruConnect is the incumbent (OEM-backed) but locked to Konecranes hardware. CraneSync's OEM-agnostic AI field service angle is the differentiator.
- CrewOS focuses on workflow/FSM, not AI intelligence. viAct is safety/computer vision, not field service assistant.
- No one is doing an "AI field service assistant for independent crane service companies" at the intersection CraneSync targets.
