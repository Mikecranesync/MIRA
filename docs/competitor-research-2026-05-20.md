## Business Context

**FactoryLM / MIRA** (factorylm.com): Turns messy maintenance reality — manuals in filing cabinets, mismatched PLC tags, tribal knowledge — into a structured Maintenance Intelligence Namespace. Then MIRA (an AI agent) runs on top to provide grounded, citation-backed troubleshooting and diagnostics to plant technicians via Slack/Telegram. Core offering: $500 assessment → $2–5K/mo pilot → $499/mo operating layer.

**CraneSync** (cranesync.com): AI-powered field service assistant and real-time monitoring platform for industrial equipment maintenance, focused on overhead cranes.

## Key Insights for FactoryLM / CraneSync

1. **The namespace gap is real and unaddressed.** No competitor combines OEM manual indexing + PLC tag reconciliation + tribal knowledge capture into a unified Maintenance Intelligence Namespace at mid-market price points. Tractian and Augury are closest but require hardware installation.

2. **The "copilot" framing is competitive.** Siemens, Augmentir, Tractian, and Nanoprecise are all calling their AI layer a "copilot." FactoryLM differentiates by being hardware-agnostic and brownfield-ready, running on existing plant documentation and OT infrastructure.

3. **CraneSync's direct overlaps:** Konecranes (brand-locked crane monitoring), Samsara (field service ops), and Augmentir (field service AI). CraneSync's advantage: multi-brand, AI-first, purpose-built for crane service technicians.

4. **YC is funding the space.** Pairo and Raven confirm institutional validation of the AI-for-frontline-maintenance niche at the early-startup level.

5. **The enterprise players (Siemens, AspenTech, PTC) are not the competitive threat.** They're too expensive and complex for FactoryLM's target market of small-to-mid industrial plants. The real competitive space is mid-market: Tractian, MaintainX, Limble, Augmentir, and Factory AI.
