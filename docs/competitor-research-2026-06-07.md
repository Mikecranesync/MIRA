## 1. Tractian
**Website:** [tractian.com](https://tractian.com)  
**Funding:** $200M total raised — $120M Series C (Dec 2024) led by Sapphire Ventures, with General Catalyst, Next47, NGP Capital  
**HQ:** Atlanta, GA (also Mexico City, São Paulo)  
**What they do:** End-to-end Industrial Copilot combining IoT vibration/temperature sensors with AI diagnostics and a built-in CMMS. Fuses hardware (Smart Trac Ultra sensors) with software — generates work orders from real-time physics-based data, integrates with SAP for parts availability. Only manufacturing-focused company on Forbes AI 50. Customers report 6–12× ROI, ~$6K saved per monitored machine per year.  
**Why relevant:** Direct competitor — full-stack AI maintenance copilot competing head-on with MIRA's industrial intelligence story. Their "Industrial Copilot" framing is almost identical to MIRA's value prop.

## 2. Augury
**Website:** [augury.com](https://www.augury.com)  
**HQ:** New York, NY (founded 2011)  
**What they do:** Machine health AI using vibration and ultrasound sensors for 200+ asset types. "Machine Health as a Service" — bundles hardware, software, and certified expert analysts. Offers Guaranteed Diagnostics™ with financial protection via Munich Re. Covers slow-rotating machinery (1–150 RPM) as of March 2025. Customers achieve 5–20× ROI. Sectors: food & beverage, chemicals, pharma.  
**Why relevant:** Gold standard for vibration/ultrasonic monitoring. Where Tractian does CMMS integration, Augury doubles down on diagnostic accuracy and expert-backed guarantees — a premium tier FactoryLM competes on reasoning quality.

## Strategic Observations for FactoryLM / MIRA

1. **The "Industrial Copilot" label is crowded.** Tractian, Siemens, and IBM Maximo all use it. FactoryLM's differentiator is the *namespace structuring* (the pre-AI data work) — lean into "Maintenance Intelligence Namespace" language rather than generic "copilot."

2. **The brownfield problem is the real wedge.** Factory AI (f7i.ai), AssetWatch, and Tractian all lead with fast brownfield deployment. FactoryLM's $500 assessment → pilot model maps onto this — emphasize the structured namespace as the brownfield unlock no one else does.

3. **Human expert overlay is a strong trust signal.** Augury, AssetWatch, and DINGO all combine AI with certified humans. MIRA's UNS confirmation gate + citation-grounded answers is the software equivalent — make that provenance story more visible.

4. **Enterprise OT ownership (Siemens, AVEVA, IBM) is the ceiling.** Large plants won't rip and replace their PI System or Rockwell stack. FactoryLM should position MIRA as the *intelligence layer on top* — a namespace that integrates with AVEVA/Maximo rather than replacing them.

5. **The YC cohort (Dropslab, RIIICO) is coming for the frontline.** AR + digital twins will hit the maintenance floor in 2–3 years. CraneSync's field service angle is well-positioned here. Watch for convergence.
