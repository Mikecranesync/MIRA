## Business Context

**FactoryLM / MIRA** (factorylm.com) is an AI-powered industrial maintenance intelligence platform that structures messy maintenance reality — OEM manuals in filing cabinets, mismatched PLC tags, tribal knowledge — into a Maintenance Intelligence Namespace (UNS/ISA-95 compliant), then runs the MIRA AI copilot on top to deliver grounded, cited answers to technicians on the floor. Pricing: $500 assessment → $2–5K/mo pilot → $499/mo operating layer. Telegram + web + CMMS write-back.

**CraneSync** (cranesync.com) is an AI-powered field service assistant and real-time monitoring platform for industrial equipment maintenance, starting with overhead cranes.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Most dangerous competitors:** Tractian and Factory AI (f7i.ai) share MIRA's exact positioning — brownfield, mid-market, fast deploy — but Tractian has $196M in funding. Watch them closely.

2. **The YC threat:** Praxis AI is backed by Y Combinator with very similar "engineering copilot" framing. Early stage but could scale fast with YC network.

3. **CraneSync's biggest threat:** Konecranes TRUCONNECT already has sensors on millions of cranes globally. The OEM IoT lock-in is the primary moat to compete against.

4. **White space MIRA owns:** None of the competitors combine (a) documentation indexing, (b) UNS/namespace structuring, (c) PLC tag reconciliation, and (d) a grounded AI copilot in one product. This is a defensible differentiation if MIRA executes.

5. **Contact approach:** For partnership/GTM conversations, Tractian, Augury, Makula, and ServiceMax are all actively expanding. Praxis AI (YC) and Factory AI are open to ecosystem conversations at this stage.
