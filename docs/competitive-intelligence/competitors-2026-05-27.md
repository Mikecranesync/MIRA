# Competitive Intelligence Report — AI Industrial Maintenance
**Generated:** 2026-05-27 | **Scope:** FactoryLM / MIRA + CraneSync competitive landscape

---

## Business Context

**FactoryLM / MIRA** (`factorylm.com`) turns messy maintenance data — OEM manuals in filing cabinets, mismatched PLC tags, tribal knowledge — into a structured Maintenance Intelligence Namespace. MIRA then runs on top as an AI-powered diagnostic chatbot grounded in that namespace (RAG over 68,000+ OEM documentation chunks). Sold as a $500 assessment → $2–5K/mo pilot → $499/mo operating layer. Target: plant maintenance technicians.

**CraneSync** (`cranesync.com`) is an AI-powered field service assistant and real-time monitoring platform for industrial equipment maintenance, starting with overhead cranes.

## Strategic Positioning Notes

- **FactoryLM's defensible moat:** The Maintenance Intelligence Namespace — nobody else is doing the structured ingestion + UNS layer as a *precursor product* to AI. Competitors bolt AI on top of an existing CMMS; FactoryLM *builds the foundation AI needs.*
- **CraneSync's gap to exploit:** Versatile owns construction, VerveTronics owns hardware monitoring. The field-service-assistant + AI-diagnostic layer for industrial cranes (service technicians in the field) is white space.
- **Most dangerous near-term competitor for MIRA:** MaintainX CoPilot — same AI-on-OEM-manuals positioning, massive install base, and strong marketing.
- **Enterprise threat:** IBM Maximo adding watsonx AI. Large plants may standardize on Maximo + watsonx rather than adopting MIRA — partner, don't compete here.
