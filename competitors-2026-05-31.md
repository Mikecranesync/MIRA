## Your Business Context

**FactoryLM (factorylm.com)** — Maintenance digital transformation: structures messy plant reality (manuals, PLC tags, tribal knowledge) into a Maintenance Intelligence Namespace, then runs MIRA (AI maintenance copilot) on top. Slack-first, Telegram-first AI assistant grounded in UNS/asset hierarchy. Pricing: $500 assessment → $2–5K/mo pilot → $499/mo operating layer.

**CraneSync (cranesync.com)** — AI-powered field service assistant and real-time monitoring platform for industrial cranes (starting with overhead cranes).

- **Maintenance Intelligence Namespace** — structuring the messy plant reality before AI touches it. Nobody else does the UNS/asset namespace structuring + OEM manual indexing + PLC tag reconciliation as a service.
- **68K+ OEM documentation chunks indexed** — Allen-Bradley, Siemens, ABB, Schneider, Yaskawa, Mitsubishi, Rockwell, Honeywell. This is a moat.
- **Grounded AI** — MIRA cites manual sections + work order history + live data. Competitors hallucinate without this foundation.
- **Price wedge** — $499/mo operating layer vs. IBM Maximo ($100K+/yr) and Emerson service contracts.

**Biggest threats:**
1. TRACTIAN ($180M, fast-growing, similar full-stack vision)
2. MaintainX (strong brand, AI copilot, $50M+ war chest)
3. Siemens Industrial Copilot (OEM ecosystem play in Siemens shops)

**Biggest opportunities (partnership/acquisition targets):**
- Averroes.ai and vHive for CraneSync (complementary, not competing)
- Fabrico.io (similar vision, different geography/stage — potential acqui-hire)
