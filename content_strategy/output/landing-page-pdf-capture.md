### Below-Fold: What's in the Guide

**The 5 steps to turn a machine photo into a working interface:**

1. **Take the photo** — Any phone or webcam. Blurry, dim, off-angle is fine. Capture the labels and nameplates.

2. **Prompt the AI** — Feed the photo + one sentence: "Reproduce this operator panel as an Ignition Perspective View; list the controls and indicators you see." (We give you the exact template.)

3. **Review the generated screen** — Check the layout, labels, and controls. The AI transcribes handwritten text — you verify it.

4. **Bind to tags** — Map each indicator to a real PLC tag (or a simulator tag) so the screen is live, not just a pretty picture.

5. **Make it diagnose** — Add one rule: "If this tag faults, turn this component red." Now it tells you something. Now it works.

Each step includes worked examples, the exact prompts, and the gotchas we hit the first time.

## Copy Notes

**Voice adherence:**
- No "AI-powered," "seamless," "cutting-edge," or "leverage" — see LinkedIn series notes.
- Language is active and simple ("Turn a photo" not "Leverage photos to create"). 
- Trust line emphasizes "maintenance guy" credibility, not product superiority.

**CTA clarity:**
- One primary CTA per page: the PDF capture form.
- Footer CTA is a soft nudge to the paid offer, not a competing ask.

**Promise specificity:**
- The page promises "5 steps," "real hardware," "real HMI," "real PLC tags" — all verifiable, not theoretical.
- Avoids "AI will do it" framing; emphasizes "you stay in control" and "you verify it."

**Email tone:**
- Opening line ("Here's the guide.") lands value immediately, no preamble.
- The soft ask ("what machine are you trying to interface?") opens a dialogue, not a sales funnel — matches the practitioner-peer voice.
- Closing is first name only, no signature block — casual, approachable.
