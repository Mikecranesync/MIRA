## Your Business (Source: factorylm.com)

**FactoryLM** turns messy maintenance reality — manuals in filing cabinets, mismatched PLC tags, tribal knowledge — into a structured **Maintenance Intelligence Namespace** that AI can actually use. Their product **MIRA** (Maintenance Intelligence Resource Agent) runs on top of that namespace to answer grounded, cited troubleshooting questions in real time.

- 68,000+ OEM documentation chunks indexed (Allen-Bradley, Siemens, ABB, Schneider, Yaskawa, Mitsubishi, Rockwell, Honeywell)
- Three go-to-market tiers: $500 Assessment · $2–5K/mo Pilot · $499/mo Operating Layer
- Built for Slack-first plant floor use, structured on ISA-95/UNS hierarchy
- CraneSync.com (returned no content — appears to be a related/prior project)

## Competitive Landscape Summary

| Category | Key Players | FactoryLM Advantage |
|---|---|---|
| **Enterprise EAM/APM** | IBM Maximo, SAP PM, AVEVA, GE Digital, Honeywell Forge | Speed to value — 1-day assessment vs 6-month implementation |
| **AI-Native CMMS** | MaintainX, UpKeep, Limble, Fiix, OxMaint, Factory AI | Namespace-first — structures the data before running AI on it |
| **Sensor + AI** | Tractian, Augury, Nanoprecise, Fluke Reliability | Software-only deployment — no hardware purchase required |
| **Enterprise AI** | C3.ai, AspenTech, SparkCognition, Uptake, Sight Machine | SMB-accessible — starts at $500 vs $100K+ enterprise contracts |
| **IIoT Platforms** | PTC ThingWorx, Samsara | Maintenance-specific focus — not a generic IoT platform |
| **Strategic acquisitions** | Brightly/Siemens ($1.6B), Fiix/Rockwell, Prometheus/Genstar | Independent — no vendor lock-in, works alongside existing tools |

**FactoryLM's clearest differentiation:** The namespace structuring step. Every competitor assumes the data is already clean. FactoryLM starts from the reality that it isn't — and makes their money building the foundation that makes AI work.
