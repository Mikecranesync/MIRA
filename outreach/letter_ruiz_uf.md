**MIRA** (Maintenance Intelligence & Response Assistant) is a working AI co-pilot for factory floor diagnosis. A technician sends a photo of faulted equipment through their existing messaging app — Telegram, Slack, Teams, or WhatsApp — and MIRA identifies the equipment via computer vision, reads the nameplate and fault codes, then walks the technician through a structured diagnostic conversation using what we call Guided Socratic Dialogue.

**The gap I'm trying to close:**

Manufacturing plants are losing institutional knowledge faster than they can replace it. Senior technicians retire and take 30 years of diagnostic pattern recognition with them. Junior techs are left with a CMMS system that tells them *what* happened and manuals that tell them *how to fix* things — but nothing that helps them figure out *what's wrong* in the first place. MIRA fills that gap.

**Why UF and why you:**

UF's manufacturing and AI research programs are well-positioned to provide the rigorous validation framework that MIRA needs to move from a working prototype to a commercially credible product. Specifically, I'm looking for a university research partner who can:

- Research optimal human-AI interaction patterns for skilled trades workers
- Bring UF's industrial engineering and AI expertise to bear on the knowledge base architecture
- Publish results that give the product academic credibility in the industrial AI space

UF's proximity to Central Florida's manufacturing base also makes field research logistics practical — I can get you access to real factory floors.
