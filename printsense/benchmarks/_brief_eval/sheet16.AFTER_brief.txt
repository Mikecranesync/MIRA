📋 Sheet 16 — the signal-output/interconnect terminal strip (-X4) of Sensor Control Unit 2 (SCU2), the sensor-monitoring wiring point.

This sheet documents terminal strip -X4 inside Sensor Control Unit 2, where sensor-monitoring status signals are landed and routed to other sheets. Terminals LOK1–LOK4 (positions 2–5) carry 'Sensor Unit 5/6/7/8 Broken' status and go to a 'DA5' module on sheet 10. BEL (position 6, from German 'belegt' = occupied) reports 'Occupied Downstream' to the DA5 module (sheet 10.5) and also branches to terminal X4.6 on sheet 15.5. PP1–PP3 (positions 7–9) route to another module on sheet 20. Positions 1 and 10 are signal ground (Gnd). This is an interconnect/terminal sheet — the sensor logic itself is on the referenced sheets, not here.

🩺 If you're chasing a fault
To trace 'Sensor Unit 7 broken' (LOK3), take two discriminating measurements: (1) at terminal -X4:4 on this strip, and (2) at the DA5 module input on sheet 10.2 (DA5 pin 2). If the expected state is present at -X4:4 but does NOT appear at the DA5,2 input on sheet 10.2, the fault is in the wiring/cable between the terminal strip and the DA5 module. If the state is already absent/incorrect at -X4:4 itself, the fault is upstream in the sensor unit / SCU logic feeding the terminal — which is NOT detailed on this sheet, so follow it to the source sheets. Note: this sheet does not show the controller's response logic, so no claim about what DA5 does is made here.
