📋 Sheet 18 of drawing 31971 (Sensor Control Unit 2, =UA2 +SCU2) — the stator over-temperature monitoring circuit using Klixon thermal switches.

This sheet documents four stator temperature-monitoring channels (ME05–ME08). A 230 V supply (230V_2, coming from sheet 4.8) is distributed across a terminal strip -18/X2KL. For each channel the supply is carried out through a 2-core cable to a Klixon thermal switch mounted at the stator, and the return conductor comes back to the terminal strip and up to a DA6 monitoring module input (cross-referenced around sheet-column 12). A Klixon is a normally-closed thermal switch: while the stator is cool it passes the loop signal; if a winding overheats the Klixon opens, the loop breaks, and the DA6 input drops out to flag the over-temperature. 🩺 If you're chasing a fault
For an ME05 over-temp indication that will not clear: with the circuit isolated, locked out, and verified dead, measure continuity across the Klixon at its field terminals +EXT-3B105.1 to +EXT-3B105.2 — when cold and healthy it should read near 0 Ω; an open reading means the switch itself (or a genuinely hot/failed stator). If the switch checks good but you get no continuity through the loop from -18/X2KL:1 to -18/X2KL:2, the fault is in cable -W5483 or its terminations, not the Klixon. Those are two distinct measurement points: the field terminals isolate the device, the terminal-strip pair isolates the cable. Note: what the DA6 module does with this input is not shown on this sheet, so do not assume its response here.
