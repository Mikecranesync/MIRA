📋 Sheet 5 'PLC overview' of Sensor Control Unit 2 (SCU2) — the Beckhoff EtherCAT I/O station and its two fiber/LAN media converters that link the panel to remote sensor units SCU1 and SCU3.

This sheet lays out the EtherCAT I/O rack: a bus coupler (-5/A100, read as EK1100) with a string of terminal modules — one 8-channel digital output (EL2008), two 8-channel digital inputs (EL1008), a 4-channel PT100/RTD temperature input (EL3204), two 2-channel 120...230VAC digital inputs (EL1722), and an end terminal (EL9011). The coupler is powered by 24V and 0V (from cross-references on other sheets) and earthed via PE. Its two Ethernet ports (ETH In / ETH Out) run to two Metz Connect media converters (-5/U1, -5/U2), which carry the network out over cables -W5445 and -W5473 ('LAN outdoor') to the remote units SCU1 and SCU3. The actual per-channel wiring is on the referenced detail sheets (/5.4, /6.4, /7.4).

🩺 If you're chasing a fault
Suppose SCU1 drops off the network. Check the link at two distinct points. First, at the coupler side of converter -5/U1: confirm the copper Ethernet from -5/A100 'ETH In' is live/linked at -5/U1. If there is no copper link there, the fault is between the coupler and U1 (patch/port), not the outdoor run. Second, if the copper side at -5/U1 is good but the converter's outgoing side / cable -W5445 shows no link at the far end (+SCU1/5.3, -ETH.D), the fault is the media converter -5/U1 or the -W5445 run to SCU1. A dead copper link at U1 points to the coupler/patch; a good copper link with no far-end link isolates the converter or its outbound cable. Note: this sheet is only the physical/overview layout — it does not show the control logic, so it cannot tell you how the PLC application reacts to the loss.
