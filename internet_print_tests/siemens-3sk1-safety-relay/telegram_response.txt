📋 Manual page 128 — two typical application circuits for a Siemens 3SK1 two-channel safety relay driving contactors Q1/Qn and a motor M (Table 7-8 E-STOP with monitored start; Table 7-9 with autostart).

These are wiring examples for a 3SK1 safety relay (types 3SK1112 Standard solid-state, or 3SK112/3SK122 Advanced). A two-channel safety sensor with two normally-closed contacts (e.g. an EMERGENCY STOP in Table 7-8) feeds inputs IN1 and IN2. The unit sends test pulses out of T1/T2 and reads them back on IN1/IN2 so a short between the two channels (cross-circuit / cross-fault) is detected — this is enabled by DIP switch 2 = ON. When both channels are healthy and closed, the relay energizes and its enable output picks up two contactors, Q1 and Qn, which each switch three phases to motor M; using two contactors gives the redundancy needed for PL e / SILCL 3. The only difference between the two circuits is the start behaviour: in Table 7-8 DIP 1 is ON = 'Monitored Start' (operator must press a start button and the relay checks the Q1 feedback contact before enabling), while in Table 7-9 DIP 1 is OFF = 'Autostart' (the output enables automatically once inputs are healthy). Supply is applied to A1 (+) / A2 (-) across the L+ / L- rails.
