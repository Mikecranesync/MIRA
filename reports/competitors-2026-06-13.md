## About FactoryLM / MIRA

**FactoryLM** turns messy maintenance reality (manuals in filing cabinets, mismatched PLC tags, tribal knowledge) into a **Maintenance Intelligence Namespace** — structured, AI-ready infrastructure. **MIRA** is the AI assistant that runs on top: grounding every answer in the customer's actual factory context (UNS paths, OEM manuals, fault history, work orders). Commercial model: $500 assessment → $2–5K/mo pilot → $499/mo operating layer. 68,000+ chunks of OEM documentation indexed (Allen-Bradley, Siemens, ABB, Schneider Electric, Yaskawa, Mitsubishi, Rockwell, Honeywell).

**CraneSync** appears to be a separate product/brand in the portfolio (cranesync.com did not return content — likely under construction or private access).

## Market Summary for FactoryLM / MIRA

### Competitive Positioning
FactoryLM's most direct white space: **mid-market discrete/hybrid manufacturing** (50–500 person plants, 1–10 production lines) that has **Allen-Bradley or Siemens PLCs** and **aging OEM documentation** — a segment too small for C3.ai/Emerson/AspenTech and not yet served by the CMMS players (MaintainX, Limble) with industrial-depth AI.

### Three Tiers

**Tier 1 — Direct CMMS competitors** (fight for the same budget):
MaintainX, Limble CMMS, OxMaint, Fiix/Rockwell, Tractian

**Tier 2 — Industrial AI platform competitors** (converging from adjacent angles):
Augury, Avathon, Sight Machine, Seeq, C3.ai, Oden Technologies, Nanoprecise, Bruviti, Dingo, DataProphet

**Tier 3 — Enterprise/infrastructure players** (channel opportunities or future acquirers):
Emerson/AspenTech, Honeywell Forge, AVEVA, Velotic, Inductive Automation, Samsara, Project Prometheus

### Biggest Opportunity Signal
The $12B Project Prometheus raise at $41B valuation (June 11, 2026) confirms: **AI for the physical world is the largest infrastructure bet of 2026.** FactoryLM sits precisely at the junction of the OT data layer and the AI intelligence layer — a position that every Tier 3 player is trying to reach from the top down.
