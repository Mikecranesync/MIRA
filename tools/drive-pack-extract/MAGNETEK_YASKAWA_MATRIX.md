## Evidence for / against (G+ Mini)

**Supports the relabel (strongly_inferred):**
- Yaskawa acquired Magnetek's *Drive Products* division in 2001 (~$28–30M) — the historical OEM
  transfer that makes a relabel plausible. (controleng.com, drivesncontrols.com)
- `CIMR-VU4A` is definitively Yaskawa V1000 (Yaskawa spare-parts BOM). Reseller listings
  (Amazon/eBay) show units dual-titled "Yaskawa … V1000 … Magnetek" / "Impulse G+ Mini … CIMR-VU4A".
- Parameter *shape* matches Yaskawa V1000: dotted groups `A/b/C/d/E/F/H/L/o/U` — the real manual uses
  `H01.01`, `U01.10`, `C12.05` (verified by us against the PDF, not the research summary).

**Against / unresolved (why not confirmed):**
- No **official** Yaskawa or CMCO document names IMPULSE G+ Mini ↔ CIMR-VU4A.
- Magnetek manual is silent on Yaskawa; post-2015 CMCO relationship to Yaskawa is "Authorized Service
  Provider," not a stated OEM supply.
- No first-hand nameplate photo showing both brandings legibly.

## The product-critical point: **treat Magnetek as its own family**

Even where the hardware is shared, Magnetek ships **proprietary crane firmware** that DIVERGES from
the Yaskawa base — Drive Commander must NEVER substitute a generic Yaskawa V1000/G5 answer:
- **Load Check II** (continuous overload detection across the frequency range)
- **Torque Proving** + **Brake Integrity Check** (brake-release confirmation / proving)
- **Swift-Lift / Ultra-Lift** (light-load overspeed)
- Crane **travel limits** (`LL1`/`LL2` lower, upper limits) and **Safe-Torque-Off**
- Crane-specific **brake-proving fault vocabulary** (`BE2` torque proving, `BE3` brake release,
  `BE6` brake-proving alarm) — absent from a standard industrial V1000.

These divergences are exactly why the pack must be Magnetek-keyed with its own faults/params, and why
the crane-domain grading supplement (family-gated) exists.
