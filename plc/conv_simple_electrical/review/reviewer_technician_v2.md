# CV-101 Electrical Print Package V2 — Technician Review

**Reviewer role:** Industrial maintenance technician, 20 years, VFD-driven conveyors.
**Deep-dive categories:** 2 (troubleshooting readability), 5 (wire/terminal ID), 7 (PLC I/O presentation), 9 (cross-references).
**Method:** Read the rubric, gold-standard set, and style law first. Read all 5 model YAML files and the 3 machine-generated matrices. Viewed each PNG at full size, then pixel-cropped (Pillow) and independently re-rendered the PDF at 4x (PyMuPDF) wherever the printed page and the model's claimed `status` seemed to disagree, since this review grades what the artifact *shows*, not what the code intended.

---

## Headline finding (applies to every sheet — read this before the per-sheet tables)

The delivered `.png` and `.pdf` files do **not** show the dashed/solid distinction the model and the on-sheet legends promise. I confirmed this by reading the SVG source (which correctly carries `stroke-dasharray="7,4"` on every `field_verify` conductor — the model→SVG step is fine) and then pixel-inspecting the PNG and a 4x PyMuPDF render of the PDF at the exact same coordinates: **every dashed line in the SVG source renders as a solid, unbroken line in both delivered artifacts.** This is visible on the legend swatches themselves — the "VERIFIED" sample and the "FIELD VERIFY" sample are pixel-identical solid strokes on every sheet that has a legend. On a grayscale photocopy (I converted the crop to grayscale to check) the two legend swatches become **completely indistinguishable** — there is no shape or color cue left, only the red wire-number tag boxes survive, and those are a secondary/backup cue by the style law's own rule 4 ("color is NOT the primary meaning").

Root cause, read directly from `render_sheet.py` line 302–315 (`_emit()`): the PDF and PNG are both generated by handing the SVG string to PyMuPDF's SVG importer (`fitz.open(stream=svg, filetype="svg")`), then `convert_to_pdf()` for the PDF and `get_pixmap()` for the PNG — one shared, broken parse of the SVG that drops `stroke-dasharray`. The package's own `validate_model.py::check_svg_audit()` cannot catch this because it only reads the `.svg` text (which is correct) — it never opens the `.png`/`.pdf` it also ships. It also only runs against `E-003` and `E-006`; `E-005` and `E-007` are outside its loop entirely.

This is **HF2** by the rubric's own wording ("A conductor rendered visually SOLID (in the PNG **and** PDF, not just the SVG) whose model status is not verified") — confirmed on all four sheets, at full severity on E-003/E-005/E-006 (every field-verify conductor affected) and at narrow severity on E-007 (only the one field-verify conductor, `SH`, is affected — the other three links are genuinely `verified` and correctly look confident).

A second, related defect (**HF5**, "any text struck by a line") is independent in mechanism: the `wire_tag()` number-flag box is offset *upward in Y* to clear the wire it labels — which works for horizontal ladder rungs (E-005, most of E-006) but does nothing for **vertical** wire runs, where the tag sits centered directly on top of the line and the wire strikes straight through the digits. E-003 is almost entirely vertical power risers, so this hits it hard (11 of 15 tags). Confirmed by direct pixel crop, not inference.

### Verdict: **NOT APPROVABLE**

Per the rubric, any hard-fail makes a sheet — and therefore the package — NOT APPROVABLE regardless of point score. All four sheets carry at least one hard-fail. Three of four sheets also score below the 90-point bar the "APPROVABLE WITH FIELD VERIFICATION" tier would require even if the hard-fails were waived.

The underlying engineering/documentation discipline is, in places, genuinely good — E-007 in particular is the strongest wiring sheet I've reviewed from this pipeline: verified terminal-to-terminal facts, a worked non-meter verification path, an honest troubleshooting block, and zero dangling cross-references. E-005's acceptance-test prose is likewise a model of what the style law asks for. What fails this package is not the modeling discipline — it's that **the rendering pipeline silently discards the one visual signal (dashed vs. solid) the entire methodology is built on**, so the delivered PNG/PDF a technician would actually carry to the panel look considerably more confident than the model backing them. That is compounded on E-003 by a second, independent defect that makes roughly three-quarters of its wire-number flags hard to read at a glance.

### Top-5 fixes demanded before I'd sign anything

1. **Fix the SVG→PNG/PDF pipeline.** Stop relying on PyMuPDF's SVG importer to carry `stroke-dasharray` — it silently drops it on every sheet, in both output formats. Either switch renderers for the raster/PDF step, or (more robust against any future backend) pre-segment dashed lines into literal short `<line>` runs in `render_sheet.py` so "dashed" survives regardless of what parses the SVG. This single fix resolves HF2 on all four sheets.
2. **Fix wire-tag placement on vertical runs.** `wire_tag()`'s Y-offset only clears horizontal wires; on vertical risers it centers the tag directly on the line. Detect wire orientation and offset perpendicular to it. This clears HF5 on E-003 (11 tags) and E-006 (1 tag).
3. **Close the validator's blind spot.** `check_svg_audit()` only reads `.svg` text and only runs against E-003/E-006. Extend it to rasterize the actual `.png` and sample pixels along each `field_verify` wire's path for an alternating ink/gap pattern, and run it against all four (soon nine) sheets.
