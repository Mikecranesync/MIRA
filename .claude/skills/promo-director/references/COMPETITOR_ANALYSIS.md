## What CMMS competitors are doing wrong (and we exploit)

### UpKeep "Nova" launch (Sep 2025) — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=28woOmOfQuQ
- **Length:** 3:51
- **Views:** 859 (in 7 months — signal: not landing)
- **Open:** "What if you could control all your maintenance data using just your voice?" — abstract, no equipment, no fault, no number
- **Footage:** 100% UI screen capture
- **VO register:** corporate marketing voice, "chaos into clarity," "take your maintenance game to the next level"
- **Trust mechanism:** feature checklist with emojis (none of which a tech recognizes)
- **Their gap → our move:** They never show a tech, real equipment, or a fault code. We open every demo on physical context (UI of a tech mid-fault) within the first 3 seconds.

### MaintainX "5 Work Orders in 5 Minutes" — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vSEFxvfcOmk
- **Length:** 5:34, **Views:** 8.7K (best-performing CMMS UI-only video)
- **What works:** "I'll prove it in real time" — the ticking clock + speed claim is honest. They earn trust by demonstrating, not promising.
- **What doesn't:** still UI-only, manager-register vocabulary ("technician," "procedure" — close to right, not quite)
- **Their gap → our move:** They prove a SPEED claim. We prove a SPECIFIC claim ("11 seconds to wiring diagram for PowerFlex 525 F012"). Specificity > raw speed.

### Fiix Product Overview — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EW_yY6ilzig
- **Length:** 10:01, **Views:** 17K (highest in CMMS group — long-form serves evaluation phase)
- **Trust mechanism:** "15 years leading the industry" + comprehensive feature depth
- **Their gap → our move:** Long-form is fine for the evaluator who's already shopping. We need a 60s entry-point video that the evaluator's tech sends them. Different funnel position.

### Limble (customer-story format)
- **Joe Romero, Myriad Genetics:** 20–30% productivity gain testimonial
- **What works:** named customer, named company, specific metric
- **Their gap → our move:** Their testimonial is a manager-on-camera. The actual maintenance tech doesn't speak. We use named-tech quotes with title and the wrenching context.

## Predictive-maintenance vendors — what they do that we should copy

### Tractian Smart Trac vibration sensor — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ulMah82maPE
- **Open 5s:** slow-motion of motor shafts, couplings, bearings, while VO names failure modes — "unbalance, looseness, lubrication issues, wear"
- **Why it works:** the vocabulary itself is the qualifier. If you don't know what "unbalance as a failure mode" means, you self-deselect. Trust earned in 5 seconds.
- **VO register:** Brazilian-accented English, technical: "dynamic factors," "irregular conditions," "condition monitoring." Speaks to reliability engineers, not generalist managers.
- **Specific claim:** Kraft Heinz, 53% reduction in corrective motor repair on 150 HP motors, 1,000+ hours downtime avoided. **Four layers of specificity** (named customer, named asset class, named percentage, named hours).
- **Steal:** name failure modes by trade vocabulary in the first 5 seconds; close on a customer with 4 layers of specificity.

### Augury Auguscope (42 seconds) — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BkqUYNAUV2k
- **Highest engagement-per-second on the Augury channel.** 42 seconds is the right length for a trust-phase video.
- **Open 5s:** "The technician walks up to the machine and attaches the sensor with a magnet." Real hands, real motor, real plant.
- **Why it works:** brevity + real equipment + named outcome ("under 5 minutes, the technician knows exactly what the problems are and how to fix them")
- **Steal:** real equipment + hands in shot + 42-second structure for trust-phase / social videos

### Augury Ultra Low Demo — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EA8Gmjs4pGA
- **Specific claim from the video:** "At a sand refining facility, we detected a gearbox issue on a rotary kiln — loose bolt on the gearbox subframe, saving almost 4,000 hours of downtime and over a million dollars."
- **Layers:** sand refining (industry), rotary kiln (asset), loose bolt (failure mode), 4,000 hr + $1M (outcome). **This is the proof-claim template.**

## Hardware-tool vendors — the gold standard for trust

### Fluke "How to Measure Temperature with the Fluke 87V"
- **Open 5s:** "Let's go over making temperature measurements with the Fluke 87V." Mike's hands, holding the meter, probe being connected.
- **VO register:** named technician (Mike), first-person, instructional. Vocabulary: "thermocouple adapter," "Min/Max mode," "reference measurement," "relative mode" — textbook tech terms, no softening.
- **Specificity:** named product, named probe model numbers (80BK-A, 80AK-A)
- **Music:** none
- **CTA:** none. Brand credibility IS the CTA.
- **Steal:** the named-engineer-on-camera convention, no music, vocabulary unsoftened. We can't put a Fluke-style person on camera (no shoot budget yet), but we mimic the VO register.

### Milwaukee Tool "Solving Jobsite Problems"
- **Format:** workers on real construction/industrial sites describing real frustrations BEFORE the product is shown
- **Steal:** open every long-form (90s+) video on a recognizable plant frustration before introducing MIRA. The product is the resolution to a problem the viewer already lives with.

## B2B SaaS launches — what doesn't translate, and what does

### What translates:
- **Stripe Atlas hook (2016):** "Starting a company used to require being in the right place." Problem stated in 3 seconds before any brand. → Our hook should state the maintenance pain in 3 seconds.
- **Linear Quality series:** intentional slow pacing, deliberate craft signals. → For our long-form sales-enablement videos, slower cuts are fine. Not for 60s entry videos.
- **OpenAI GPT-4o launch (May 2024):** demonstration IS the pitch — the model is already talking before the pitch starts. → Our equivalent: MIRA is already answering a question before we say "MIRA is a diagnostic AI."

### What doesn't translate:
- **Vercel/Next.js Conf trailers:** 1.5–2s/shot rapid cuts. Too fast for industrial. We hold 4–5s.
- **Tesla / Apple cinematic reveals:** orchestral swells, dark backgrounds, mystery. Reads slick to industrial buyers. We use ambient minimalism and silence during demo.
- **Figma Config keynotes:** CEO-on-camera. Founder narration only works if the founder is famous; not yet for FactoryLM.
- **Dev-tool hype trailers (Cursor, Replit):** kinetic type, percussive electronic, energy-first. Burns trust with skeptical industrial buyers.

## Direct-response patterns — high-conversion structure

### Dropbox MVP video (2007, Drew Houston)
- 5,000 → 75,000 beta signups overnight
- **Mechanism:** answered one question (sync your files across machines) so clearly that viewers said "yes, I have that problem" within 10 seconds
- **Steal:** clarity beats cinema. If a viewer can say "yes I have that pain" within 10 seconds of our hook, we've won.

### Y Combinator demo day pitches (60-second format)
- **YC coaches' rule:** never start with the founding story or market size. Start with what makes investors lean forward.
- **Steal:** never start with our company history. Start with the most impressive thing the product does.

### ProductHunt #1 launches (last 24 months)
- **Wordware (#1, 7,700+ upvotes):** filmed on a laptop at 6 AM via ScreenStudio, zero editing, zero animation. **Authenticity outperformed every polished launch that week.**
- **Steal:** clarity > cinema, again. We don't need shoot budget; we need clarity.

### Hormozi offer mechanics
- **Stack components explicitly** in Reveal — three named things, not "everything"
- **Price anchor before trial** in CTA — "$97/mo. Start free for 14 days." NOT "Free trial, $97/mo after."
- **Cost of inaction visible in numbers** in Pain — "Every hour the line is down costs more than a year of MIRA."

## Convergent rules across all 30 videos

1. Start in the viewer's world by second 3 (Stripe, OpenAI, Tractian, Augury, Apple, Tesla — all converge)
2. One sentence per screen — never more than 8 words in any caption
3. Show output before input
4. Music as infrastructure, not performance
5. Single CTA — URL, date, or one word
6. Shot duration matches audience cognition (industrial: 3–5s; dev-tools: 1.5–3s)
7. Proof beats claim
8. VO register = "senior peer, not pitchman"

## Convergent anti-patterns

1. Logo → company name → "We're excited to announce"
2. Founder-narrated "Hi, I'm X"
3. Feature enumeration over single demonstration
4. Slick over real (drone shots, cinematic swells for industrial buyers)
5. Multiple CTAs
