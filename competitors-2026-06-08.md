## Your Space (Summary)

You're operating at the intersection of three categories:

1. **AI-powered industrial maintenance / IIoT** (Tractian, Augury, Nanoprecise)
2. **Next-gen CMMS with AI** (MaintainX, Limble, Makula, Fiix, UpKeep)
3. **Connected worker / frontline AI copilots** (Augmentir, Tulip, Siemens Copilot)

Your differentiator vs. all of them: **you structure the namespace first.** They assume clean data. You create it.

## Strategic Takeaways for FactoryLM / MIRA

| Quadrant | Companies | Your advantage |
|---|---|---|
| **Well-funded, high threat** | MaintainX (Autodesk), Tractian, Augury | You structure the namespace they assume exists |
| **OEM-locked copilots** | Siemens, Schneider/AVEVA, Fiix/Rockwell | You're vendor-neutral across all OEMs |
| **Mid-market CMMS** | Limble, Makula, UpKeep, eMaint | You add OEM manual KB + PLC tag layer they lack |
| **GenAI chat closest competitor** | Nanoprecise/ReKurv.ai | Theirs requires sensors; yours works from existing data |
| **Enterprise too-big** | IBM Maximo, AspenTech, Hexagon | You serve the $500 assessment → $5K pilot market |

**Biggest market signal:** MaintainX's $3.6B Autodesk acquisition validates that structured maintenance data + AI is worth billions. The acquirer is Autodesk — a CAD/BIM company. That means the game is about **structured factory context** as much as AI on top of it. That's exactly what FactoryLM builds.
