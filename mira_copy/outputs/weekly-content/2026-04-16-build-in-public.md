r/maintenance

Spent the last 3 weeks training an image model to read VFD fault codes from bad photos.

Why? Because I kept seeing techs take 4 tries to snap a usable shot of a GS20 display under shop lighting.

We tested on:
- PowerFlex 4M, 525, 755
- GS10/GS20 with dirty screens
- Hydraulic HMI panels at 15° angles

Ground truth: 98% accuracy on F3 (Overcurrent), F4 (Ground Fault), F8 (Overvoltage).

Model failed hard on glare until we added synthetic lens flare to training images.

Now it corrects light distortion and reads through fingerprints.

If you’re manually transcribing fault codes from HMIs, this might save you 20+ minutes per incident.

We’re using it in beta at two packaging plants — 437 photo diagnoses last week.

Happy to share the training dataset specs if anyone’s working on something similar.
