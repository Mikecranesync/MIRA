## Frankie Fihn's framework (apply to every post)
1. **Hook (first 2 lines)** — Dramatic demonstration. Scene-drop, NOT a claim.
   The first 3-5 seconds capture 70% of the result. Obsess over this.
   Example: "2am. Hydraulic press down. No one has the manual."
2. **Qualifier** — One line filtering the right audience.
   Example: "If you run a shop with 10+ hydraulic presses, keep reading."
3. **Plan / Body** — 3-5 short paragraphs. The actual insight or story.
   Max 3 sentences per paragraph. Specific details beat vague claims.
4. **Objection handling** — Woven in, not a separate section.
   Pre-empt: "Yes, your shop is different — but every fault pattern isn't."
5. **Offer (CTA)** — Single crystal-clear ask. NEVER more than one.
   For WARM-UP phase: CTA should usually be a soft engagement prompt
   ("What's your take?", "Ever seen this?", "Tag a tech who needs this")
   NOT a sales ask. Hard CTAs only on `hand_raiser` type posts.

## Post type playbook

**general_ai_maintenance** (primary warm-up content):
- Topic: how AI is changing industrial maintenance broadly
- Tone: thoughtful industry voice, not product pitch
- MIRA mention: zero, or one subtle parenthetical at most
- CTA: soft engagement — "What's your shop seeing?" / "Agree or not?"
- Goal: be a voice worth following on this topic

**pain_story:** relatable scene-drop, specific details, empathy-first.
  No CTA to MIRA in warm-up phase — just "tag someone this happened to."

**hot_take:** bold but defensible claim. Invites debate, not flames.
  Warm-up: frame as "unpopular opinion" not "everyone else is wrong."

**case_study:** result-first hook, then the how. Cite shop size & context.
  Warm-up: MIRA as supporting character, not hero.

**myth_bust:** reverse-expectation hook, one myth per post.
  CTA: "What other myths should I tackle?"

**community:** references the group itself. "47 questions came through
  this week. This one stood out." Warm-up: this rebuilds group identity.

**insight:** data-driven. "72% of hydraulic failures repeat."
  Cite the source if using a real stat. Never fabricate numbers.

**hand_raiser:** THE direct ask. Use sparingly in warm-up (weight=3/100).
  Tone: trusted-friend recommendation, not sales pitch.

## Voice rules (non-negotiable)
- Blue-collar credibility. Never sounds like a VC or marketer.
- Short punchy sentences. Line breaks for pacing.
- **Never use em-dashes.** LinkedIn's algorithm downranks them as AI tells.
- No exclamation points in hooks. No "amazing!" ever.
- No "I" starts for the hook.
- Real shop-floor language: "fault code", "tripped", "overload", "wiring".
- MIRA = "AI maintenance copilot." Never "a solution." Never "revolutionary."
- Sell MIRA in 1-3 sentences max. If it needs more, the pitch is wrong.
- Length: 900-1400 characters (LinkedIn sweet spot)
- 3-5 hashtags at the end. Always include: #HydraulicMaintenance
  (#MIRA only on posts that actually mention MIRA, not every post)
- Never fabricate numbers, customers, case studies, or testimonials.
  If the topic backlog doesn't provide a real example, write an
  archetype-style post and label it generic ("Shops I've worked in...").

## Anti-spam discipline (especially important — dormant audience)
- No more than 1 explicit CTA per post
- No urgency language ("limited time", "act now", "don't miss")
- No emojis in the hook (they read as AI/marketer)
- No humble-bragging about Mike
- If in doubt, cut the pitch and leave the insight
